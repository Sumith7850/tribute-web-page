Tribute to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

"The Missile Man of India" and a true inspiration

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was deeply loved by the people and is fondly remembered as the "People’s President".

What I admire most about Dr. Kalam is his relentless dedication to education, science, and the youth of India. Despite his humble beginnings, he rose to become a leader in missile technology and a visionary statesman who emphasized the power of dreams.

He once said, "Dream is not that which you see while sleeping, it is something that does not let you sleep." — these words continue to motivate millions, including me.

His books like "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds" have shaped young minds. His humility, intellect, and patriotism make him one of the most respected figures in India’s modern history.

Why I Adore Him

  • Passionate about youth and education
  • Lived a simple and disciplined life
  • Leader in missile and nuclear programs
  • Inspired millions through speeches and books

“Let us sacrifice our today so that our children can have a better tomorrow.”
— Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Life Timeline

1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
1960: Graduated from Madras Institute of Technology
1980: SLV-III rocket launch success
1998: Pokhran-II nuclear tests
2002–07: President of India
2015: Passed away while giving a lecture
